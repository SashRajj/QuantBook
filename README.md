# Cross-Sectional Signal Research

End-to-end quant research and execution stack: **data → signals → portfolio
construction → multi-strategy combination → live execution → audit**. Six
factor / ML / pairs strategies on the S&P 500 and top 30 crypto pairs,
evaluated under one framework — point-in-time membership, HAC standard
errors, Deflated Sharpe, asymmetric 5/10 bps costs, two-day execution lag —
then combined into a fund-of-strategies book wired into a broker-agnostic
order-management layer with pre-trade risk gates and an append-only audit
log.

Headline multi-strategy combination: **Sharpe 0.73 OOS net of costs**,
positive skew (+0.15), near-zero SPY beta (-0.04), -13.6% max DD over the
2021-2026 OOS window. The lift comes from inverse-vol weighting across
uncorrelated equity / crypto / pairs sleeves plus a 10% vol-target overlay —
not from search or tuning. The combination clears `|t| > 1.7` HAC against
SPY but not `|t| > 2`, and no single strategy passes a family-wise
Bonferroni bar. The README is explicit about that.

Two strategies clear the per-test `|t| > 2` HAC bar after realistic costs
and SEs: standalone optimised volume-adjusted momentum on crypto, and the
in-sample epoch of the LightGBM decile spread on the top-300 most-liquid
S&P 500 names. Both fail OOS — the documented factor-decay pattern of the
post-2020 era.

**Multiple-comparison caveat.** Notebooks 01-11 evaluated roughly 65 strategy
configurations. Bonferroni-corrected at family-wise 5%, the bar is
`|t| > 3.0`, not 2.0. Neither result above clears that bar. The honest
reading: nothing in this repo survives a family-wise multiple-testing
correction. The `|t| > 2` column is the conventional per-test threshold,
not a discovery claim.

**Crypto survivorship caveat.** The vol-mom t = 2.15 is computed on the top
30 USDT pairs by *today's* market cap. LUNA, FTT, UST and other 2021-22
blow-ups are structurally excluded from the long leg. Re-running on a
2019-vintage candidate set is on the v2 list; until then, read the t-stat
as upper-bounded.

The classical equity factors don't pass at any significance bar. **That is
the project's main result.** What the equity notebooks are good for is
calibration: a pipeline that runs survivorship-corrected data, point-in-time
membership, realistic execution lag, asymmetric costs, DSR, and HAC SEs and
reports `t = 0.21` for the strategy a no-cost backtest would report as
`t = 4.53` is a pipeline you can trust when it *does* report a positive
result.

## The three linear signals

| Notebook | Signal | Hypothesis |
|---|---|---|
| `01_mean_reversion` | Negated rolling z-score of price | Short-horizon overreaction reverts in days–weeks |
| `02_momentum` | 12-1 month total return | Slow news diffusion → 1-12 month trends |
| `03_low_volatility` | Negated rolling realised vol | Leverage / lottery preferences underprice low-vol names |

Each notebook follows the same template: hypothesis, IC at multiple horizons,
parameter grid, rolling IC, rank-weighted backtest, transaction-cost
sensitivity, optimised backtest, OOS validation, return-distribution and
tail-risk section (histogram vs Gaussian, QQ, VaR/CVaR at 1% and 5%). The
mean reversion notebook also runs SPY-beta neutralization and a Sharpe-based
grid search alongside the IC heatmap — IC and net Sharpe can disagree once
costs and covariance enter.

## Portfolio (notebook 04)

Three signals combined via cross-sectional z-score averaging, then run
through the full evaluation suite at portfolio level: cross-sectional rank
correlation between signals, time-series correlation of single-signal PnL,
combined-signal optimization (dollar-neutral, 2% position cap, 5 bps cost),
return distribution diagnostics, VaR/CVaR, drawdown depth and duration,
rolling 1y Sharpe and SPY correlation, and walk-forward over rolling
5y-train / 1y-test windows.

## Feature selection (notebook 05)

Bayesian variable selection on eight candidate features (mean reversion,
momentum, low-volatility at multiple lookbacks). Gibbs sampler hand-implemented
in NumPy using SSVS spike-and-slab. Outputs are posterior inclusion
probabilities and posterior coefficient distributions — an uncertainty-aware
view of which lookbacks earn their place after accounting for everything
else.

## Regime Monte Carlo (notebook 06)

Two-state Gaussian HMM on SPY returns to identify bull/bear regimes, then
forward paths drawn from the fitted transition matrix with strategy returns
bootstrapped conditional on the simulated regime. Reports the empirical
distribution of 1y Sharpe, max DD, and terminal wealth across thousands of
paths, plus the relationship between path Sharpe and realised bear-state
share. The bootstrap preserves within-regime fat tails; the HMM provides
the regime structure a plain bootstrap would lose.

## Crypto (notebook 07)

Same code applied to the top 30 USDT pairs on Binance.US (2019-09 →
present). What changes vs equities:

- No index-membership concept — universe is a curated list; per-date masking
  drops names with no data yet
- 365 days/year, `exec_lag=1` (24/7 market), 10 bps/side (Binance.US taker)
- Dollar-neutral via perpetual futures (implicit — spot shorting is limited);
  funding cost acknowledged but not modelled

Five candidate signals: mean reversion, momentum, low volatility,
market-residual momentum, and volume-adjusted momentum
(`mean_return * sqrt(volume / rolling_mean_volume)`). Each runs through the
per-signal optimizer and an IC-weighted combiner.

**Result**: standalone optimised volume-adjusted momentum hits Sharpe 0.83,
**HAC alpha t = 2.15** vs BTC — the only crypto strategy past `|t| > 2`.
Cross-sectional momentum is borderline at t = 1.56. The IC-weighted combined
portfolio is Sharpe ≈ 0 IS and -0.50 OOS after the look-ahead audit.

Caveats: ~6 years of history centred on the 2021-22 boom-bust, narrow
breadth (30 names vs 500), funding cost not modelled, universe curated by
today's market-cap.

## ML alpha (notebook 08)

Non-linear cross-sectional model. 16 features: the linear factor signals at
multiple lookbacks plus skew, kurt, MAX/lottery, log-ADV, price-to-200d-MA.
Target is forward 10-day cross-sectional rank. Model is LightGBM.

Two correctness pieces specific to ML on financial panels:

- **Purged k-fold CV with embargo** (López de Prado, *Advances in Financial
  Machine Learning*, ch. 7). Forward-10d labels overlap with adjacent
  training rows by construction; purge drops training rows whose label
  window overlaps the test fold, and the embargo (`HORIZON + 1` days each
  side) handles feature serial correlation.
- **Walk-forward retraining**. Train on the prior 5 years, embargo 11 days,
  predict the next year. Full panel of OOS predictions spans 2010-2026 and
  is genuinely OOS relative to the model that produced each row.

Reported on the same realistic-cost framework as the linear factors:

- IS decile spread (≤2020-12-31): Sharpe 0.99, alpha **t = 2.47** ✓, DSR 0.70
- OOS decile spread (2021-2026): Sharpe ≈ 0, t = -0.06
- Optimized (cvxpy MV, 5 bps turnover, 2% cap, dollar-neutral): Sharpe 0.26 full, ≈ 0 OOS

The IS Sharpe survives HAC + DSR adjustment for 12 hyperparameter trials,
but the OOS Sharpe is zero — exactly what an honest pipeline reports when
classical cross-sectional performance has decayed. Sharpe > 1 in both halves
would deserve suspicion.

## Pairs (notebook 09)

Statistical arbitrage on the S&P 500. Selection (2010-2017) within each
GICS sub-industry: correlation > 0.65, Engle-Granger cointegration p < 0.05
on log prices, OU half-life of the residual spread in [5, 60] trading days.
Second tier: same-sector pairs with correlation > 0.80. 33 pairs survive.

Trading (2018-2026): static OLS hedge ratio from selection window, 60-day
rolling z-score on the spread, entry at `|z| > 2`, exit at z = 0, hard stop
at `|z| > 3.5`. Each pair contributes a gross-1 position; per-pair PnL is
normalised by 120-day calibration vol, then the aggregate is scaled to a
10% annualised vol target. 5 bps/side/leg, 1-day exec lag, PIT membership
masking.

Results (post-selection, after costs):

- 2010-2017 selection window (diagnostic only): Sharpe 1.68, HAC t = 4.53
- IS 2018-2020: Sharpe 0.41
- OOS 2021-2026: Sharpe 0.64, alpha t = 1.12
- Per-year 2018-2026: -0.03, 1.34, 0.36, 2.07, -0.23, 0.63, 0.69, 0.43, 1.83

Positive but well below the Sharpe-2-after-costs that Gatev, Goetzmann &
Rouwenhorst (2006) reported on 1962-2002 data — matches the post-2010 decay
in Krauss et al. (2017). The gap between the 1.68 selection-window Sharpe
and the IS/OOS Sharpes is what overfitting in pair selection looks like
even with a stationarity test and an economic prior. A Kalman-filter
hedge-ratio variant runs as comparison and underperforms static OLS OOS —
small drift parameters track noise rather than the cointegration vector.

## Multi-strategy combiner (notebook 10)

Single-sleeve numbers are conservative. Each sleeve on its own doesn't
clear the per-test bar, but cross-correlations are small (equity vs crypto
-0.04, equity vs pairs -0.23, crypto vs pairs -0.02), so a fund-of-strategies
view captures real diversification.

`multi_strategy.py` exposes three **fixed** rules — no parameter search:

- **Equal-weight**: `w_i = 1/N`
- **Inverse-vol (risk parity)**: `w_i ∝ 1/σ_i` on trailing 60-day realised vol, `shift(1)` before applying
- **Minimum-variance**: per-date QP `min w'Σw s.t. sum(w)=1, w≥0` on 252-day rolling cov, `shift(1)` before applying

Equal-weight gets dragged down by the high-kurtosis crypto sleeve (kurt 191
on combined PnL). Both vol-aware rules cut crypto exposure and let pairs
contribute more — well-behaved tails (kurt 2-3).

A 10% annualised vol-target overlay (`helper.vol_target`, 60-day trailing
realised vol, `shift(1)`, leverage capped at 5x) is applied to the inverse-vol
combination. Vol-targeting helps inverse-vol but not min-variance — min-var
already produces stable variance by construction; an overlay on top is
redundant.

| Configuration | Sharpe | Skew | Kurt | HAC t | Max DD | Ann vol |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Equal-weight | 0.04 | -8.12 | +191 | 0.32 | -27.9% | 18.10% |
| Min-variance | 0.70 | -0.09 | +2.41 | 1.45 | -8.3% | 8.08% |
| Inverse-vol | 0.64 | +0.07 | +2.86 | 1.63 | -11.4% | 8.56% |
| **Inverse-vol + 10% vol-target** | **0.73** | **+0.15** | **+2.23** | **1.73** | **-13.6%** | **10.62%** |

Headline combined OOS Sharpe net of 5 bps/side: **0.73**, positive skew
(+0.15), near-zero SPY beta (-0.04) — the first positive-skew portfolio in
the repo. Still doesn't clear per-test `|t| > 2` (HAC t = 1.73) and not
Bonferroni. But the result is honest, diversified, and stable enough to
size a real (if small) book against.

Honest read on the lift: most of it is diversification across uncorrelated
sleeves; the vol-target overlay adds ~0.09 to Sharpe by reallocating risk
across time. None of this is search-found — the three rules are textbook
and the overlay is the standard 60-day inverse-vol scale.

## Options alpha (notebook 11)

Cross-sectional equity sleeve where the signal is options-derived: ranks
S&P 500 stocks by the per-name variance risk premium (model-implied vol
minus realised vol) and trades long-short dollar-neutral. The strategy
trades the underlying, not options. Greeks enter via the Black-Scholes
pricer underlying the surrogate IV, vega-weighted position sizing, and
ex-post Greeks-based PnL attribution.

The IV input is a GARCH(1,1) per-name variance forecast plus a 20%
multiplicative VRP constant (Bollerslev, Tauchen, Zhou 2009). Free
historical OptionMetrics-grade IV is not available for S&P 500 single
names; the surrogate is the binding constraint, not the framework. When
GARCH persistence is high the surrogate is near-affine in trailing
realised vol, so the signal can overlap with notebook 03's low-volatility
factor when both are run.

Universe is top-150 by pre-IS (2005-2007) dollar volume — smaller than
notebook 08's 300 to keep GARCH refits tractable. The signal sign is
flipped from the literature direction (Bali-Hovakimian 2009 / Goyal-Saretto
2009) after IS IC inspection; that binary choice is counted as a trial
in `N_TRIALS = 16` for DSR.

Results: IS Sharpe +0.18 (HAC alpha t = 0.48, DSR 0.008), OOS Sharpe +0.12
(HAC alpha t = 0.25). Neither half clears `|t| > 2`. The sleeve is
uncorrelated to the existing three (-0.002 to equity, -0.006 to crypto,
-0.091 to pairs), but the +0.12 OOS Sharpe is too low to lift the
inverse-vol headline combination, so `multi_strategy.py` stays
3-sleeve. The notebook ships as a methodology demonstration: BS pricer,
closed-form Greeks, GARCH IV surrogate, vega-weighted sizing, full
PIT / HAC / DSR realism — the surrogate IV is the binding constraint.

## Execution layer

The bridge from research to a (paper) trading account. Purpose is to
demonstrate the research-to-live pipeline is in place and correct, not to
claim profitability. The equity book's OOS HAC t is 0.16, so live-paper
PnL is expected to be flat-to-negative net of costs. That's the point — a
pipeline that reports faithfully is more useful than one that overstates.

### Pipeline

```
combine_weights.latest_target_weights(asof)   # research-side weight vector
        │
        ▼
runner.run                                     # daily entry point, cron target
        │
        ├─ price_feed.fetch_latest_close       # live prices via yfinance
        ├─ price_feed.feed_health              # hard-fail on stale feed
        ├─ price_feed.reconcile                # broker vs internal book
        │
        ├─ risk.PreTradeRiskGate.check         # gross/net, position cap, ADV, drawdown
        │
        ├─ execution.weights_to_target_shares  # weights × equity / price → shares
        ├─ execution.build_order_list          # diff vs current positions
        ├─ execution.execute_market/twap/vwap  # parent/child slicing
        │
        ├─ execution.implementation_shortfall  # bps gap from decision price
        └─ execution.AuditLog                  # append-only JSONL of every event
```

### Modules

- **`execution.py`** — broker-agnostic OMS. Abstract `Broker`, `PaperBroker`
  simulator (deterministic fills, configurable slippage/commission), and an
  `AlpacaBroker` stub showing the live-integration shape. Each `Order` has
  an idempotent `client_id` (re-submitting returns the prior broker id with
  no second fill — how a retry survives a network blip), a status state
  machine (`PENDING → SUBMITTED → PARTIALLY_FILLED/FILLED/CANCELED/REJECTED`),
  and an audit trail. The `PaperBroker` invariant — cash + position market
  value + realised PnL = starting cash − commissions — is tested end-to-end,
  including the four cross-zero cases (long→flat, long→short, short→long,
  short→flat) where a miscoded realised-PnL sign is the most common bug.

- **`risk.py`** — `PreTradeRiskGate` runs every order basket through:
  drawdown kill switch (equity vs HWM), gross/net leverage, per-name notional
  cap, max position count, ADV-participation cap per order, daily turnover
  cap, optional blocked-symbol list. Any single breach rejects the *entire*
  basket — partial rebalances leave the book in a state nobody asked for.
  Limits live in `config.yaml`.

- **`price_feed.py`** — pulls the latest adjusted close per symbol from
  yfinance and computes a feed-health record (last-bar age vs SLA). Stale
  feed hard-fails the rebalance; runner exits non-zero so cron alerts.
  Staleness is *not* handled by silently shrinking positions via alpha decay
  — that conflates "market hasn't moved interestingly" with "our data
  pipeline is dead", and the second one costs money. `reconcile()` compares
  prices and positions between internal book and broker; persistent drift
  is the most common silent failure in real systems.

- **`combine_weights.py`** — research-to-execution bridge. Loads the price
  panel, computes the three factor signals from `signals.py`, runs
  `helper.ic_weighted_combine`, feeds into `helper.Optimizer` with the same
  parameters as notebook 04 (dollar-neutral, 2% per-name cap, 5 bps cost
  penalty). Exposes `latest_target_weights(asof)` for the daily runner, plus
  `apply_signal_decay(signal, age_bars, half_life)` — decay applied
  signal-side because post-optimisation weight decay violates the constraints
  the optimiser solved under.

- **`runner.py`** — the cron target. Loads config, builds today's weights,
  fetches prices, runs feed-health, builds the risk gate from 20-day ADV,
  calls `execution.rebalance`, appends to `logs/YYYY-MM-DD/journal.jsonl`.
  Supports `--dry-run` and `--asof YYYY-MM-DD`.

### Why pairs and crypto aren't in the live runner

- **Pairs** is event-driven (z-score crossings of a cointegrated spread,
  per-pair sizing, held to exit threshold). The current `Broker` and
  `rebalance` abstractions are date-driven; bolting pairs in would corrupt
  the design or duplicate it badly. A pairs-execution module with stateful
  per-pair entry/exit logic is a clean v2.
- **Crypto** uses Binance.US data and a 24/7 market with different cost
  and execution conventions; co-mingling into an Alpaca-targeted weight
  vector would create more bugs than lines saved. A separate
  `runner_crypto.py` against the Binance.US testnet is the right split.

### Broker choice

Default is `paper` — no signup, no API keys, runs offline (yfinance is the
only external call). To swap in Alpaca paper trading: install `alpaca-py`,
populate `.env` with `APCA_API_KEY_ID` / `APCA_API_SECRET_KEY`, uncomment
the `AlpacaBroker` body in `execution.py`, set `config.yaml: broker.type =
alpaca`. `runner.py` runs unchanged — the `Broker` interface is the
decoupling point.

## Optimizer

The `helper.Optimizer` solves a per-date mean-variance program with cvxpy:

```
minimise   -alpha' w + w' Σ w + λ ||w - w_prev||_1
```

Subject to optional constraints: dollar neutrality, per-name position cap,
gross leverage limit, long-only, ex-post vol scaling. Covariance is
Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage on a trailing window. The transaction-cost penalty
enters the objective directly, so the optimizer trades signal strength
against rebalancing cost rather than producing unimplementable weights.

## Realism corrections

Where free data allows, the project addresses the usual paper-strategy
inflation sources:

- **Point-in-time index membership.** `download_sp500.py` reconstructs the
  S&P 500 from Wikipedia's historical changes table. Union of all
  ever-members forms the download universe (~830 tickers vs 500 today). The
  optimizer takes a date-by-date membership mask so positions only open in
  names actually in the index on that date.
- **Execution lag.** `port_ret` defaults to `exec_lag=2`: a signal computed
  at the close of day t is acted on at t+1 and earns t+1 → t+2.
- **Asymmetric transaction costs.** Separate bps on long/short turnover plus
  a daily holding fee on the short book at a configurable borrow rate.
- **Deflated Sharpe Ratio.** `helper.deflated_sharpe` /
  `probabilistic_sharpe` (Bailey & López de Prado 2012, 2014) penalise the
  headline Sharpe for parameter trials and for skew/kurtosis of the realised
  PnL.
- **Implausible-return filter.** `clean_prices` masks any same-day return
  greater than 100% in absolute value — catches adjusted-close errors
  yfinance returns for delisted/acquired tickers.
- **Point-in-time covariance.** Cov refits on a trailing window (default 252
  trading days, refit monthly) inside `run()`, not once on the whole panel
  in `__init__`. Tests assert that perturbing future returns no longer
  changes past weights.

## Remaining limitations

- **~175 delisted tickers lack yfinance data.** Membership mask still
  records them on the relevant dates, but the strategy can't trade them.
  Residual survivorship bias reduced, not eliminated.
- **Single-factor risk model.** Ledoit-Wolf is reasonable for a few hundred
  names but isn't a multi-factor structural model. Position limits and
  dollar neutrality compensate in part.
- **No market-impact cost.** Transaction cost is per-bps of turnover, no
  square-root or non-linear size dependence.
- **Daily, close-to-close.** Intraday latency, opening auctions, and
  tick-level effects are out of scope.

## Why these three linear signals

They span distinct theoretical categories so the portfolio notebook has
something to combine:

- Mean reversion — contrarian, short-horizon noise
- Momentum — trend-following, behavioural underreaction
- Low volatility — risk-based anomaly, marginal-investor frictions

They are textbook factors. The point of the project is the evaluation
template around them, not the signals themselves.
